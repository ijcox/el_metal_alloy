Solder
A low-melting metal alloy used to join metal components, especially in electronics, plumbing, and metalwork.

Solder is a fusible metal alloy that melts at relatively low temperatures and is used to create permanent bonds between metal parts. Unlike welding, soldering does not melt the base metals being joined — only the solder material itself.

Solder plays a critical role in electronics manufacturing, circuit board assembly, plumbing connections, jewelry making, and metal fabrication.

Common Types of Solder

Lead-Based Solder

Traditionally made from tin and lead (Sn-Pb). Easy to work with but largely phased out due to toxicity concerns.

Lead-Free Solder

Typically made from tin with silver and copper (SAC alloys). Used in modern electronics to meet environmental regulations.

Silver Solder

Contains silver for stronger joints. Common in jewelry and higher-strength applications.

Plumbing Solder

Designed for water pipes. Must meet potable water safety standards.

Key Properties

  • Low melting temperature
  • Good electrical conductivity
  • Good thermal conductivity
  • Ability to wet and bond to metals
  • Moderate mechanical strength

Applications

  • Printed circuit board (PCB) assembly
  • Electrical wire connections
  • Plumbing pipe joints
  • Stained glass assembly
  • Jewelry and metal crafts

Soldering Process

  • Clean metal surfaces
  • Apply flux to prevent oxidation
  • Heat joint using soldering iron or torch
  • Apply solder to heated joint
  • Allow joint to cool and solidify

Flux is essential for removing oxides and ensuring proper adhesion.

Advantages

  • Lower temperature than welding
  • Simple and cost-effective
  • Ideal for delicate electronic components
  • Creates reliable electrical connections

Disadvantages

  • Lower mechanical strength compared to welding
  • Lead-based types are toxic
  • Can weaken under high heat

Safety Considerations

  • Use ventilation to avoid inhaling fumes
  • Avoid contact with molten solder
  • Wash hands after handling lead-based solder
  • Use temperature-controlled equipment
Solder is a foundational material in electronics and metal joining, enabling strong, conductive connections without melting the base materials.
